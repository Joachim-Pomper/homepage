Welcome to my webpage!
My name is Joachim and I am currently a physics Master student at the University of Graz, busy with finishing my master thesis about a class of composite dark matter models. I use this website to provide some information about me, my studies and future research interests, as well as to display a few projects I did in the past. Feel free to browse a little. About me

In 2016 I enrolled in Technical University Graz to study physics. I had great fun studying undergraduate physics, both theoretically and experimentally. Surprisingly I discovered my fascination with abstract mathematics during my bachelor studies and I thus continued to pick up several additional classes from the mathematics bachelor program.
At the end of my physics bachelor 2019, I began to work part-time for a company, developing automation and control systems for renewable energy grids. During that time I took additional courses in mathematics on measure theory, numerics and functional analysis.
In 2020 I started a masters program at the University of Graz with focus on quantum field theory and gravity. For my master thesis I work on a theoretical model of strongly interacting dark matter and its phenomenological implications. While I had multiple positions as a teaching assistant since my third semester, my favorite one was the preparation of lecture material for a course on differential forms in electromagnetism. This was the job that got me interested in differential geometry and further brought me back to general relativity. In winter 2022 I took a little break to travel to Australia, where I was invited to present some results of my masters thesis at DSU 2022. Currently, I am writing up my master thesis, while looking for a PhD position.
