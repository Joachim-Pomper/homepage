About me

I am a Masters student at the University of Graz. The focus of my master studies was on Quantum Field theory and Gravity. Currently I am working on my masters thesis, where I study a class of QCD-like gauge theories in order to modell a SM-extension of strongly interacting dark matter. During this project I came in touch with cosmology and astroparticle physics. My future research interest however lies at the intersection of QFT and Gravity, with focus on physics beyond perturbation theory. Especially when it comes to quantum gauge theories there is a lot of non-perturbative aspects we yet do not fully understannd. To only name a few we have the Gribov ambiguity, extended field configurations or the phenomena of Confinement.
Lattice QFT appears to me to be a tool that might help us make severe progress in the understanding of these topics. But there also exists other non-perturvative tools like Dyson Schwinger Equations,the Functional Renormalization Group approach or Holography. While I would like to learn more about each of them, especially the last one, using the structural similarities between Gravity and QFT, is of great interest to me.
I also always had a fable for Quantum Field Theory in curved spacetime. Recently the algebraic apporach caught my attention. Most of the work done in this area however is of perturbative nature. In my opinion, algebraic quantum field theory pottenially provides a framework to deal with physical issues, for example the notion of particles or antimatter in a curved spacetime background, while simultanious offering a mathmathical rigorous description of QFT. Of course with the caveat of beeing a semi-classical description, not taking into account quantum-gravity effects. The question if such semi-classical descriptions are valid at all in order to adress for example the information-loss-paradox of Black Holes remains an open question of research. One to whose answer I would be glad to contribute to in the near future.
