Hello there!

My name is Joachim, and I am a first-year PhD student at the Università di Pisa. For my Bachelor and Master education I studied within the NAWI Graz Physics progamm, offered by the Karl Franzens University (KFU) and the Technical University of Graz (TU Graz). I concluded my sutides with a masters thesis on theorethical aspects of a specific class of strongly interacting dark matter theories, which I defended in July 2023.
My research interests revolve around aspects of fundamental physics, quantum field theory, and gravity. For most of my academic life, I was primarily involved in theoretical, almost mathematical, physics. In particular, I have a weakness for differential geometry and the use of differential forms. However, during my master's research, discussions with various experienced physicists slowly changed my perspective. While I remain fascinated by the ingenious ideas that fellow theoretical physicists have developed over the last decades, my PhD focuses on the reasonable question: "But can we measure this?"
And since the hype is real, I jumped onto the gravitational waves train. Using the new and current detectors as my observational tool of choice, I try to make predictions on exciting theoretical hypotheses in fundamental physics and develop new tools to eventually test them. My deep understanding of the involved theory has turned out to be quite helpful, while I have also learned about many interesting challenges on the observational side. At this stage, I really enjoy the balance between working with theory and observational/simulated data.

The Road So Far

Sep-2024: Recipient of the ÖPG Student Price 2024

won the Student Prize of the ÖPG for my master's thesis work, which awarded me a unique cup and €750. Four potential awardees were selected from the applicants based on their thesis work, but the final decision was made through a competition in science communication. You can find the video related to the final phase on Youtube.

Feb-2024: First Paper on the ArXive

After my master's thesis, I refined my results a bit further and finally submitted them to SciPost, hoping it will be accepted as my first publication. The preprint version is available on ArXiv. This project has been quite the journey.

Feb-2024: Research visit Stavanger

Following my master's research, I was offered a fully funded research visit at the University of Stavanger, by Helena Kolešová, to give a seminar on my masters work and discuss further directions for the research on strongly interacting dark matter togehter with her and her PhD student — a trip I thoroughly enjoyed.

Nov-2023: Started my PhD in Pisa

I won the PhD Call B at the University of Pisa with the grant "Theoretical Methods for Detecting New Physics Signals." The position came without a fixed project or supervisor, but with a lot of creative freedom. Although it was a big risk, I decided to accept the position, hoping it would allow me to pursue my own research interests.

Jul-2023: Master thesis defence

After a bit more than a year of hard work, I successfully defended my master thesis on a particular theory of strongly interacting dark matter. As part of the exam, I had to choose two subjects for a public oral exam. My choices were gravity and quantum field theory. I am proud to say I graduated with distinction.

Sep-2022: My first talk at an international conference

I won a competition at my university that allowed me to attend a conference abroad with a total budget of up to €3000. To make the most of the opportunity, I traveled to the Dark Side of the Universe conference (DSU2022) in Sydney, Australia. I'm proud to say that my talk was extremely well received. I will never forget how people treated me completely differently before and after my talk—many did not take me seriously beforehand. After the conference, I stayed for an extra month during the winter vacation. Great times!

Sep-2022: My first talk at a conference

I presented the status and preliminary results of my master's thesis at the 71st Annual Meeting of the Austrian Physical Society (ÖPG2022). The conference was an extremely nice experience.

Mar-2022: My first summer school at GGI

I participated in a summer school on the theoretical aspects of astroparticle physics, cosmology, and gravitation at the Galileo Galilei Institute (GGI). There, I had my first lecture on the subject of gravitational waves and early universe phase transitions. Due to the aftermath of the COVID-19 pandemic, I could only participate online.

Feb-2020: My favorit teaching assistant position

For this position, I was not supervising any students but was organizing lecture material for Professor Stefan Kurz in the form of presentation slides for his course on differential forms in electromagnetism. This has been my favorite position so far. Although I had never attended a course on the subject, Professor Kurz entrusted me with this task and incorporated many of my ideas on how to present certain topics to the students.

Okt-2020: Enrolled in NAWI Graz Physics Master program

The NAWI Graz Physics Master program was shared between Karl Franzens University (KFU) and the Technical University of Graz (TU Graz). My studies focused on theoretical high-energy physics, i.e., quantum field theory and gravity.

Sep-2020: Bachelor thesis defence

I successfully defended my bachelor thesis on the Onsager strategy to calculate the spontaneous magnetization of Ising ferromagnets by mapping the problem onto a free fermionic field theory.

Okt-2019: Student trainee at BEST - Bioenergy and Sustainable Technologies

I began to work 10 hours part-time at Best and received my own little project at the company, where I focused primarily on testing and developing software for hydraulic and thermal simulations for buildings and district heating grids. I really enjoyed working with the local team and often considered to join them again.

Okt-2017: My first teaching assistent position

I was very fortunate to have the opportunity to supervise an exercise class for first-year students on the subject of linear algebra, which was one of my favorites at the time.

Okt-2016: Enrolled at TU Graz in Technical Physics

After completing my civil service in Austria, I decided to pursue my interest in mathematics and, especially, physics, and enrolled in a Bachelor’s program in Technical Physics at the Technical University of Graz (TU Graz).
